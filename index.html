The Problem
Worth Solving

IMDb is the world's largest film database — 200M+ users, backed by Amazon, the most comprehensive entertainment dataset ever assembled. Yet it is losing the generation that will define the next decade of media consumption.

Gen Z discovers films through TikTok's FYP. They manage watchlists in Notes apps. They share opinions on Letterboxd. They find streaming availability on JustWatch. IMDb — theoretically the one platform that could serve all these needs — gets used only to check a rating, then abandoned.

This project asks a simple question: What if IMDb actually designed for the way people experience film?

The redesign is not about cosmetics. It is a fundamental rethinking of IMDb's product strategy — from a database you query to a platform that understands you, discovers with you, and helps you decide what to watch in under 60 seconds.

The result: a full UX sprint covering research, competitive analysis, personas, IA, user flows, and a production-grade interactive prototype across desktop and mobile — built with the exact colour palette and typography specified in the design brief.

02
UX Research
What's Broken
& Why It Matters

Heuristic evaluation, competitive analysis, and secondary research revealed eight core problems — each mapped to a severity level and a UX law.

Critical
Discovery is Dead
No mood-based filtering, no intelligent recommendations, no serendipity. IMDb functions as a catalog you already know — not a guide to what you don't know yet.
Hick's Law
Too many paths, no clear entry
Critical
Cognitive Overload on Every Page
Movie pages dump cast, crew, trivia, parental guides, reviews, and awards simultaneously — no hierarchy, no progressive disclosure, no breathing room.
Miller's Law
No grouping or disclosure
Critical
The Decision Paradox
IMDb's core job: help me decide what to watch. Yet there is no "worth watching" verdict, no streaming availability, no single CTA. Users leave to Google the answer.
Peak-End Rule
Journey ends in frustration
Critical
Aggressive, Disruptive Advertising
Ad density is among the highest of any media platform. Ads interrupt reading, hijack scroll, and break mobile layouts — alienating exactly the premium users advertisers want.
Aesthetic-Usability Effect
Perceived quality collapses
Moderate
Zero Meaningful Personalisation
The homepage is identical for a first-timer and a 10-year power user. Ratings and watchlists are collected but barely leveraged. The platform knows you but acts like it doesn't.
Recognition over Recall
No personalised shortcuts
Moderate
No Community or Social Layer
Reviews exist in isolation. No way to follow critics you trust or see friends' activity. Letterboxd built its entire competitive advantage on this gap alone.
Social Proof principle
Missing entirely from product
Moderate
Mobile is an Afterthought
The mobile app compresses desktop content rather than rethinking it. Dense tables, small tap targets, horizontal overflow. Gen Z users abandon on first friction.
Fitts's Law failure
Small targets, poor reach zones
Low
Dated Visual Identity
The yellow-black palette carries connotations from 2005. Gen Z describes IMDb as "functional but ugly." The brand has zero cultural currency with the next decade's audience.
Aesthetic-Usability Effect
Negative brand perception
"IMDb is data-rich but experience-poor. It knows everything about every film — and almost nothing about the person watching it."

The White Space — Golden Opportunity

No single platform combines: comprehensive database + community + mood-based discovery + streaming availability + cinematic UI in one place. Each competitor owns one piece. The redesigned IMDb is the first platform to own all five simultaneously — and that is the unfair competitive advantage.

Key Design Decisions
UX · Discovery

Mood Picker as Primary Home CTA

The primary entry to discovery is emotional, not categorical. Six moods replace 20+ genre options — reducing cognitive load from an overwhelming menu to an intuitive emotional choice.

Hick's Law · Emotional Design
UI · Film Page

Sticky Quick Decision Box

A persistent sidebar showing: rating, runtime, age rating, audience verdict, and streaming availability. Answers "should I watch this and where?" in under 5 seconds — kills the #1 exit behaviour.

Peak-End Rule · Fitts's Law
UX · Information Architecture

Tabbed Movie Page (Not Scroll)

Five progressive tabs: Overview, Cast, Reviews, Media, Details. Each serves one specific intent. No user is ever confronted with more information than they sought.

Miller's Law · Progressive Disclosure
UX · Retention

Spotify-Style Smart Playlists

Watchlists become named, shareable, scheduled playlists with auto-generated covers. Transforms a forgotten feature into a personal curatorial project users return to.

Endowment Effect · Social Proof
UI · Mobile

Horizontal Mood Scroll on Mobile

The mobile Mood Picker is a horizontally scrollable chip row — always visible, no tap needed to reveal. Mirrors TikTok's content-type row which Gen Z users already understand intuitively.

Jakob's Law · Recognition over Recall
UI · Visual Identity

Monochromatic Palette + Cinematic Grain

The strict monochromatic palette with a single gold accent creates editorial restraint associated with Criterion and A24 — brands the target audience actively aspires to. A noise texture overlay adds filmic quality.

Aesthetic-Usability Effect

01

Emotion is a Valid Navigation System

The insight that mood should be the primary entry to discovery came from observing how people actually talk about wanting to watch something. Nobody says "I want a genre-drama film." They say "I want something that makes me feel something." Design should meet users in their natural language.

02

The Competition Gap is a Design Opportunity

Running a structured competitive audit revealed that every competitor excels at one thing and fails at everything else. IMDb's actual competitive moat — its database — is underutilised. The opportunity was not to build something new, but to use what already exists in a smarter, more human way.

03

One Clear Question Per Screen

The biggest structural failure of current IMDb is asking users to answer multiple questions simultaneously on every screen. The redesign enforces a single primary question per view: "What do you feel?" on Discover, "Should I watch this?" on Film Page. Clarity of purpose drives engagement.

04

Visual Identity is a Product Decision, Not a Cosmetic One

The shift to a monochromatic editorial palette is not aesthetic preference — it is a deliberate positioning decision. The gold/black IMDb palette signals "database tool." The new palette signals "cultural institution for serious film lovers." Brand perception directly affects willingness to engage, share, and pay.
